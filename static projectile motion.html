Projectile Motion Experiment

Introduction

Projectile motion refers to the motion of an object that is projected into the air under the influence of gravity. It follows a curved path known as a trajectory. This path is influenced by the object's initial velocity and launch angle.

Key Equations

The motion of a projectile can be described by the following equations:

Max Height: H = (v² * sin²(θ)) / (2 * g)

Range: R = (v² * sin(2θ)) / g

Where:

  • v = initial velocity
  • θ = launch angle
  • g = gravitational acceleration (approximately 9.81 m/s²)
